Future of Technology

There have been many MANY big tech advancements over the past few years that will have the power to shape the future of the world as we know it. From nano tech to 3d printed houses to brain-implanted chips, the progression of technology has skyrocketed beyond what we could have ever dreamed of.

And yet, some people don't even know the first thing about it. That's what this is for. This website will not only teach you about recent technology advancements, but will also show you just how far we have come, and it'll do it in a fun way (or at least try to). So go on and click on one of the topics below that interests you and be amazed!

Humanoid_Robots

Humanoid robots are something that has popped up more recently because of the advancements in technology. A humanoid robot is just that, a human-like robot. Humanoid robots currently can't fully replicate human movement, but they can get pretty dang close.

Some have things like AI object recognition that can detect objects on the ground and what they are, while others can breakdance. China is even developing a bot that can act as an artificial womb for babies.

So far, the possibilities of this technology seem endless, but remember, if something seems too good to be true, it is. So check out the humanoid robot to see just how cool and just how scary these metal guys(and girls) are.

Quantum_Computing

Quantum computing has been around for a decent bit of time, but has been popping up much more recently because of researchers making multiple breakthroughs and developments that could reshape how we see the future. That's why it is one of the topics we will go into on this website.

A quantum computer has much more computing power than any classical computer does and ever will. Take Microsoft's Majorana 1 Chip for example. It alone not only has more processing power than all the computers on earth combined, but it can also fit in the palm of your hand.

However, quantum computing has lots of problems that can make or break the use of quantum computers. So go to the Quantum computing page and check out all the good and bad of this earth-shattering technology.
